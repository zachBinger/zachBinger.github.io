Fantasy Football
Scoring Formats

Evaluating the impacts
of PPR on runningback scoring

PPR is becoming a popular scoring format, however, it is still a controversial discussion with FFB purists. The format is believed to inflate the scoring of the position and add exagerated value to players that would otherwise have none. Players such as Tarik Cohen, James White, and Duke Johnson are some of the first to come to mind. The figure below shows the scoring for the top 50 ranked RBs for each scoring format. Additionally, the bar plot shows the differential of the player's scoring between the two formats.

The figure shows that the "trend" of RB scoring is uneffected with a smooth logaritmic decay and instead is just shifted up approximately 40 points. However, Christian McCaffrey is broke in this scoring format with and insane 469 fantasy points. Already the #1 back in standard formats, the gap between McCaffrey and the 2nd scoring back grew from an already stuoid 73.6 pts to a mindfucking 150.4 pts. If the PPR format has no effect on creating a more dynamic point distribution among the players there may not be any positive value to the format. Conversely, players who were once lower ranked in standard formats are now among the heigher tiers of RBs. Players such as:

  • Royce Freeman
  • Tarik Cohen
  • Duke Johnson
  • Latavius Murray
  • James White
  • Jamaal Williams
  • Devin Singletary
    • All propelled themselves from unstartable into the RB3 category.
